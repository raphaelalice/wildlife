Wildlife Habitats

Discover natural homes of Tanzania’s wildlife

Forest Habitat

Tanzania’s forests are rich in biodiversity. Key regions include the Amani Nature Reserve in Tanga, Uluguru Mountains near Morogoro and the Eastern Arc Mountains. Forests are home to primates, birds, and countless plant species.

Animals found: Colobus monkeys, Sykes monkeys, Birds, Insects

Savannah Habitat

Savannahs dominate northern Tanzania, especially in Serengeti National Park, Ngorongoro Conservation Area and parts of Tarangire National Park. These grasslands support large herbivores and predators.

Animals found: Lions, Zebras, Wildebeest, Giraffes, Elephants

Wetland Habitat

Wetlands include rivers, lakes, and swamps such as Lake Manyara National Park, Pangani River Delta, and Selous Game Reserve. They provide breeding grounds for fish, birds, and crocodiles.

Animals found: Hippos, Crocodiles, Fish, Flamingos, Water birds

Semi-Arid & Desert Habitat

Semi-arid regions are found in Dodoma, Lolkisale, and parts of Kitulo Plateau. Animals here have adapted to scarce water and high temperatures.

Animals found: Camels, Snakes, Lizards, Small mammals like rats and moles
