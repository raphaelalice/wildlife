Why Wildlife Protection Matters

Tanzania is home to some of the world’s most diverse wildlife, including elephants, lions, rhinos, and giraffes. Wildlife protection supports biodiversity, tourism, and preserves the nation’s natural heritage for future generations.

Key Wildlife Laws in Tanzania

Wildlife Conservation Act, No. 5 of 2009

The principal law governing wildlife conservation, hunting regulations, protected areas, and penalties for wildlife crimes.

National Parks Act

Establishes and protects national parks to ensure wildlife habitats remain undisturbed.

Ngorongoro Conservation Area Ordinance

Balances wildlife conservation with the livelihoods of local communities.

Environmental Management Act, 2004

Regulates environmental activities to prevent damage to wildlife habitats.

Illegal Wildlife Activities

  • Poaching and illegal hunting
  • Trading wildlife products (ivory, horns, skins)
  • Destruction of wildlife habitats
  • Unauthorized possession of protected wildlife
